Discover Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh lies on India’s southeastern coast — a state of culture, history and natural variety. From the ancient pilgrimage center of Tirupati and the Buddhist relics of Amaravati to the sun-kissed beaches of Visakhapatnam and the cool hills of Araku Valley, Andhra offers spiritual journeys, heritage trails, delicious spicy cuisine and scenic escapes.

The state is known for classical arts (Kuchipudi dance), temple architecture, fertile river plains (Krishna & Godavari), and vibrant handicrafts like Kalamkari. Andhra’s festivals and rituals blend devotion and celebration, making every visit culturally rich.

Ideal time to visit: October to March. Explore coastal drives, temple circuits, hill-station coffee trails, and UNESCO-level archaeological sites. Andhra is perfect for pilgrims, beach-lovers and heritage seekers.

Top Tourist Places in Andhra Pradesh

Tirupati

  • Tirumala Venkateswara Temple
  • Silathoranam (natural rock arch)
  • Alamelu Mangapuram
  • Local prasadam & temple experience

Visakhapatnam (Vizag)

  • RK Beach & Lighthouse
  • INS Kurusura Submarine Museum
  • Kailasagiri Hill Park
  • Araku Valley (nearby)

Araku Valley

  • Borra Caves
  • Tribal coffee plantations
  • Protected natural trails & waterfalls

Amaravati

  • Amaravati Buddhist Stupa & Museum
  • Historic ruins & riverfront drives
  • Local handicrafts and markets

Srisailam

  • Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple
  • Srisailam Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Horsley Hills (nearby in Andhra/Telangana border)

Other Famous Attractions

  • Ryali Temple & Pushkarini tanks
  • Konaseema & Godavari Delta (green backwaters)
  • Borra & Katiki beaches
  • Kondapalli Toys & Kalamkari workshops
