Discover Assam

Assam, in India’s northeast, is a vibrant land of the Brahmaputra river, rolling tea gardens, ancient temples and unique tribal & Ahom heritage. From the world-famous Kaziranga National Park (home of the one-horned rhinoceros) to the river island Majuli and the sacred Kamakhya Temple, Assam offers varied experiences — wildlife safaris, cultural festivals, serene river life and unique cuisine.

The state’s history blends Ahom rule, Vaisnavite culture and tribal traditions. Assam’s festivals — especially Bihu — are full of music, dance and color. The tea gardens of Jorhat and Tinsukia produce the famous Assam tea that’s celebrated worldwide.

Nature lovers will enjoy wetlands and birding sites, river cruises on the Brahmaputra, and trips to the hills of Haflong. Assam is a peaceful but lively destination — ideal for culture seekers, photographers, and eco-tourists.

Plan visits between October and March for comfortable weather, abundant festivals and easy wildlife sightings.

Top Tourist Places in Assam

Guwahati

  • Kamakhya Temple (Nilachal Hill)
  • Umananda Temple (Peacock Island)
  • Assam State Museum
  • Fancy Bazaar & Brahmaputra riverfront

Kaziranga & Jorhat

  • Kaziranga National Park (UNESCO, one-horned rhino)
  • Tea gardens around Jorhat & Dibrugarh
  • Majuli access points & cultural satras

Majuli

  • River island satras (Vaishnavite monasteries)
  • Traditional mask-making & pottery
  • Birdwatching & river life

Sivasagar

  • Rang Ghar (Ahom amphitheatre)
  • Talatal Ghar
  • Ahom-era temples and tanks

Haflong & Hills

  • Haflong Hill Station
  • Scenic viewpoints & local tribal markets

Other Famous Attractions

  • Manas National Park (Bodo & UNESCO)
  • Tea Museum (Jorhat)
  • Dhola-Sadiya Bridge viewpoint
  • Ali-Aye-Ligang (Mising festival) experiences
