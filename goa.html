Discover Goa

Goa, India’s smallest state by area, is world-famous for its golden beaches, Portuguese-influenced architecture, spice-scented cuisine and lively festivals. From the party beaches of North Goa to the tranquil coves of South Goa, the state offers everything — water sports, heritage trails, wildlife escapes, and laidback coastal vibes.

History, culture and nature blend in Goa — visit UNESCO-style churches like the Basilica of Bom Jesus, explore 17th-century forts, trek to Dudhsagar Falls, or simply relax with a cup of fresh seafood and a sunset over the Arabian Sea.

Best time to visit: November to March (pleasant weather, festival season). For quieter beaches, visit in shoulder months.

Popular Spots in Goa

Baga Beach

Famous for parties, water sports and beach shacks.

Fort Aguada

17th-century Portuguese fort with panoramic ocean views.

Palolem Beach

Beautiful crescent beach — calm, scenic and perfect for retreats.

Dudhsagar Waterfalls

Majestic waterfalls — best seen during/after monsoon season.

Goa offers beach resorts, boutique stays, heritage homes and budget guesthouses. Savor local Goan cuisine in seaside shacks and Portuguese bakeries. Remember to respect local rules on beaches and nightlife.

🍛 Must-Try Goan Delicacies

Goan Fish Curry

Coconut-based tangy and spicy curry — must-try seafood.

Vindaloo

Fiery curry with Portuguese influence — try the pork variant.

Bebinca

Traditional layered Goan dessert — rich and sweet.

Feni

Local liquor made from cashew or coconut — Goan specialty.
